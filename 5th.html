Poonjar Palace, located in the Kottayam district of Kerala, is a historical monument that showcases traditional Kerala architecture. Here's a detailed description:

Historical Background

  • Relocation: In 1152 AD, the Poonjar dynasty relocated from Madurai due to civil wars and established their kingdom in the Meenachil River valley
  • Dynasty Origins: The Poonjar dynasty traces its lineage back to the Pandyan Kings of the Sangam Age, with Manavikrama Kulasekara Perumal as its founder.
  • Construction: Built around 600 years ago by the Poonjar Rajas, who were linked to the Travancore royal family.

Architectural Features

  • Furniture and Antiques: The palace houses an impressive collection of antique furniture, including grand chandeliers, jewelry boxes, palm leaf engravings, and various lamps
  • Design: The palace features intricate carvings and sculptures of Hindu deities, reflecting exceptional craftsmanship
  • Materials: Constructed primarily from wood, the palace is an excellent example of traditional Kerala architecture
