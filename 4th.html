The Edakkal Caves are a fascinating archaeological site located in the Wayanad district of Kerala, India. Here's a detailed description:

Location and Geology

  • Geology: The caves are not technically caves but rather a natural rock shelter formed when a large boulder got wedged between two bigger boulders. The name "Edakkal" means "a stone in between"
  • Location: Situated on the Ambukuthi Hills, approximately 25 kilometers from Kalpetta and 12 kilometers from Sulthan Bathery

Historical Significance

  • Cultural Links: Some of the carvings suggest a possible connection to the Indus Valley civilization, particularly the "man with jar cup" motif
  • Discovery: The caves were discovered by Fred Fawcett, a police official of the erstwhile Malabar state, in 1890
  • Age: The Edakkal Caves date back to the Neolithic era, with some carvings believed to be over 8,000 years old
